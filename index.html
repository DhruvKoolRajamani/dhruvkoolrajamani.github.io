About

I've been tinkering with robots since Middle School with the advent of the LEGO Mindstorms robot. Supplementing my LEGO building skills with basic functional programming tools I began experimenting with microcontrollers through DIY Projects available online.

Fast forward to college, I continued expanding my interest in Robotics by joining a budding Robotics Team - Mars Rover Manipal and by the end of my first year, the team and I had designed our very own Mars Rover Prototype.

I cultivated my interest of Biomechanics through my internship at the BioRobotics Lab at EPFL where I was introduced to a plethora of bioligically inspired Robots. Inspired by the projects, I've created a group in my University that which serves as a community for scholars to share their research and ideas, as well as a platform to teach others our knowledge of Robotics.

Enough about me, come take a look at my Robots.

Autonomous Robotics Lab, IITD

Underactuated Flexible Manipulator

Designed a 3-link and 4-link planar manipulator on MATLAB and implemented a nonlinear control theory called Differential Flatness, allowing the end effector on the manipulator to follow a desired trajectory with just 2 non-colinear input forces.

What's Next?
I hope to extend this theory to n-links and actually make a physical robot to validate my simulations.


A 4-link manipulator actuated by 2 non-colinear input forces, following a circular path.

Mars Rover Manipal


Best Rover team from Asia (2017)
We participate regularly at the University Rover Challenge

Mars Rover

Developed a Mars Rover prototype that flaunts a modified rocker-bogie suspension, low pressure balloon tires, custom designed scientific testing mechanism, 6-DOF Robotic Arm and can run autonomously for as long as an hour.

Robotic Arm Lead

Other than developing the entire Rover, I was in charge of designing a 6-DOF Robotic Arm that fit the competition criteria with added features such as a self-adapting parallel jawed gripper and a modular assembly (for easy replacement of parts).

Our team stood 8th out of 82 teams at the URC, 2017
